## 2. Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

### ICP A: Worker-Side (The Gig Pro)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Active US gig worker earning primary or supplementary income from 1+ platforms |
| **Platforms** | Uber, DoorDash, Lyft, Instacart, Amazon Flex, Upwork, Fiverr, Grubhub |
| **Income** | $20K–$120K/yr from gig; has been gig-working for 6+ months |
| **Pain Point** | Denied credit card, apartment, auto loan, or personal loan because bank "can't verify income" |
| **Current Workaround** | Tries to show bank statements, 1099 forms, or platform earnings screenshots — often rejected |
| **What They Want** | A way to prove their income is real, consistent, and growing — to get same access as W-2 workers |
| **Tech Profile** | Smartphone-native. Familiar with gig apps, mobile banking, possibly crypto-aware (Coinbase). |
| **Psychographics** | Proud of their hustle. Frustrated by systemic bias against gig work. Willing to try new fintech if it solves access. |
| **Geography** | Urban / suburban US. Higher density in California, Texas, Florida, New York — but nationwide. |
| **Age** | 22–45 |

### ICP B: Lender-Side (The Forward-Thinking Underwriter)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Credit risk manager, head of consumer lending, or innovation officer at a bank, credit union, or fintech lender |
| **Organization** | $500M–$50B AUM institution seeking to serve the 64M gig workers |
| **Pain Point** | Losing 20%+ of applicants who have solid income but no W-2 — leaving money on the table. Current models (FICO, VantageScore) miss these borrowers entirely. |
| **Current Workaround** | Manual review of bank statements (slow, expensive, fraud-prone). Some use Argyle/Plaid for raw data but lack scoring model to action it. |
| **What They Want** | A turnkey solution: standardized gig-worker credit score + full verification reports, with clear regulatory compliance path. |
| **Adoption Barrier** | Must be cheaper than manual review. Must integrate via API or shareable link (no heavy engineering). Must comply with FCRA, ECOA, and UDAAP. |
| **Decision Trigger** | Seeing approval rates increase 15-30% on gig-worker segment without additional loss. |
| **Geography** | US nationwide. Target: agile fintechs first (credit-first neobanks, BNPL players) then community banks/CUs |

## 4. Three Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: INCOME IS INCOME — Gig Earnings Are Real Earnings

**Why it matters:** The entire gig economy faces a systemic bias: 64M workers earn reliably but are treated as unverifiable. Krostio eliminates this discrimination through hard data.

**Proof points:**
- Connects directly to Uber, DoorDash, Lyft, Upwork, Instacart, Amazon Flex, Grubhub, Fiverr APIs
- Aggregates earnings across all platforms into a single verified profile
- Krost Score (300-850) — the first creditworthiness score built exclusively from gig income signals

**Messaging angles:**
- "Your 1099 is not a liability. It's proof of income."
- "Uber drivers deserve credit score alternatives too."
- "Verify your income — not your employment status."

**Tone:** Empowering, facts-driven, slightly defiant of the status quo.

---

### Pillar 2: YOUR DATA, YOUR PASSPORT — Self-Sovereign Financial Identity

**Why it matters:** Workers are tired of their data being siloed in platforms they don't control. Krostio's on-chain Passport gives them a portable, verifiable credential they own forever.

**Proof points:**
- Soul-bound token on Base L2 (low gas, fast settlement, Ethereum security)
- Cannot be revoked by any platform or employer
- Shareable link + PDF report — works without the recipient needing crypto

**Messaging angles:**
- "Your gig history should leave when you do."
- "A credit file you actually own."
- "No bank vault. Your chain, your passport."

**Tone:** Future-forward, tech-optimistic, sovereignty-minded but accessible (not jargon-heavy).

---

### Pillar 3: LEND SMARTER — Turn Gig Workers Into Your Best Customers

**Why it matters:** Lenders are leaving 20%+ of addressable market unserved. Krostio gives them the data + score + compliance to safely lend to gig workers.

**Proof points:**
- 9-factor scoring model designed for gig income volatility
- PDF verification reports (standardized, underwriter-ready)
- Shareable links — no integration required for basic verification
- REST API for deep integration

**Messaging angles:**
- "The single biggest untapped credit market in America."
- "Stop guessing. Start scoring."
- "Gig workers already have income data. Now they have a creditworthiness score."

**Tone:** ROI-driven, analytical, confident — tailored for risk managers and product VPs.

## 6. Brand Voice Guidelines

### Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| **Validating, not patronizing** | Gig workers are tired of being treated as second-class. Never talk down. Never say "side hustle" as a default. |
| **Confident, not arrogant** | Krostio is new. Acknowledge the legacy of traditional credit without mocking it. |
| **Tech-forward, not tech-jargon** | We use on-chain technology but we don't lead with it. Lead with benefit, explain tech as needed. |
| **Bilingual (worker + lender)** | The brand must speak to gig workers AND underwriters. Different channels get different registers. |

### Voice Spectrum

| Channel | Register | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Consumer app / marketing** | Warm, empowering, first-person plural | "We built Krost Score because gig workers deserve real credit. Period." |
| **Lender sales / docs** | Professional, data-driven, ROI-focused | "Institutions using Krostio see 22% higher approval rates on previously thin-file gig applicants." |
| **Social media** | Witty, punchy, community-minded | "W-2 or 1099 — income is income. Let your earnings speak." |
| **Crypto-native channels** | Builder-first, technical credibility | "Krost Passport: soul-bound on Base L2. Your off-chain income, on-chain verified." |

### Words to Use vs. Avoid

| ✅ Use | ❌ Avoid |
|---|---|
| Gig worker, pro, earner | Side hustler, gigger (minimize) |
| Income verification | Income guessing |
| Credit score alternative | "FICO killer" (adversarial) |
| Worker-owned | Self-sovereign (jargon) |
| Verification link | Magic link (generic) |
| On-chain passport | NFT (confusing for mainstream) |
| Platform | Protocol (unless B2B crypto) |

### Tone Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Do speak to the frustration of being denied despite reliable income
- Do use concrete numbers (64M workers, 300-850 score, 7 platforms)
- Do explain the "why" before the "how" (benefit before blockchain)
- Do show empathy for lenders' compliance burden (Krostio handles it)

**DON'T:**
- Don't say "disrupting credit" — everyone says this
- Don't make workers feel like charity cases (they have income — banks are the problem)
- Don't lead with crypto unless the audience is crypto-native
- Don't overpromise — Krostio improves access, it doesn't guarantee approval
