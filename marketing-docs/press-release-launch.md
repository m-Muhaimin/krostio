## The Problem

Traditional credit scoring — FICO, VantageScore — measures debt repayment behavior. It was built for W-2 employees with predictable paychecks and years of credit history. For gig workers, the system fails in three ways:

1. **No W-2, no score path.** Lenders ask for pay stubs. Gig workers don't have them.
2. **Irregular income is penalized as risk.** The CFPB found that income variability is treated as a credit risk indicator even when total annual earnings match or exceed salaried peers.
3. **Single-platform snapshots miss the picture.** A driver who works Uber AND DoorDash has income resilience no single-platform data source captures.

The result: gig workers earning $50K–$100K/year are denied credit at rates 3x higher than salaried employees with equivalent income.

---

## How Krost Works

1. **Workers connect their platforms** — Uber, DoorDash, Lyft, Upwork, Fiverr, Instacart — via read-only OAuth.
2. **Krost computes a 300–850 score** from 12 months of verified earnings data using a four-factor model: income stability (30%), average monthly income (25%), platform diversity (15%), and earnings trajectory (15%).
3. **The score is attested on Base L2** — a permanent, publicly verifiable record that cannot be altered, retracted, or lost.
4. **Workers share their score** with any lender via a single authorization link. Lenders receive a verified score and on-chain attestation hash. No raw financial data is shared.
