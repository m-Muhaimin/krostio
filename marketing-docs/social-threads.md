## LinkedIn Post (Lender Audience)

**Format:** Thought leadership, 300-500 words

**Headline:** The $1 trillion market most lenders are ignoring

**Body:**
64 million Americans do gig work. They earn over $1 trillion annually. Most can't get approved for credit because the system was built for W-2 employees.

Here's what I've learned talking to credit union executives and fintech lenders:

The bottleneck isn't willingness to lend. It's income verification.

There's no standardized way to verify gig income that lenders trust. So they fall back on "bank statements" — which are noisy, slow, and invasive.

The fix isn't a better verification API (Argyle/Plaid exist). The fix is a persistent, standardized credit score built from gig income data — that any lender can query without re-verifying the worker every time.

Think of it like FICO for the gig economy. Same 300-850 scale. Same lender trust. But based on real earnings data, not debt repayment history.

If you're lending to underserved consumers and want to figure out how to serve gig workers: this is the infrastructure piece that's been missing.
